Susanne Jung — 2023
Artist website

UX UI RESPONSIVE WEBSITE ART DIRECTION HTML

Susanne Jung is a contemporary artist working in Berlin.

Susanne Jung‚Äôs work follows a painting style that integrates color-field painting in an abstract and minimal form. She explores simple geometric arrangements and experiments with the effects of colors, forms, lines and surfaces.

I redesigned Jung's website, reorganized her works into clear categories, and curated her visual brand positioning in the digital sphere.

My responsibilities: UX Research, UX Design, Information Architecture, Prototyping, Art Direction, UI Design, HTML

Full project online: susannejung.de

The Goal


Jung has expressed the main goal of the website is to display her artworks online, and use it as a contact channel for users interested in her teaching workshops.



The Challenge


How can I improve the digital experience of Jung's website in a way that properly displays and communicates the value of her work, while easing on the user's journey exploring her artistic world?

The Research


Research methods applied:
  •  3 x Interviews (1 Stakeholder, 1 SME, 1 User)
  •  3 x Usability testing
  •  Business and competitive Research

The SME (a professional art curator) expressed the importance of browsing through chronological art development when reviewing artists' works online. In addition, being able to review used techniques and explanation of the artistic decisions.

Define and Ideate


Define & Ideation Methods applied:
  •  Information architecture
  •  Site map


Adding a large number of artworks to the website has created an information architecture challenge. Low-fi prototyping and testing, as well as talks with the artists about her artistic vision, resulted in the solution of organizing the artworks by categories, with a focus on the high-demand technique to be presented first.

The Design


Exploring Jung's work and artistic inspiration online lead to my art direction decision to curate the website in a minimal and geometric way. I was strongly inspired by indie art website designs and gallery editorial design grid for the website's layout.

The Outcome


The new website is addressing both main goals of the website. All works are displayed in a clean and minimal manner, and organized by categories and chronological order. In addition, Jung‚Äôs contact details and professional references are all found on the same info page, with a contact form.

Testing the website has generated positive feedback from both the artists and users.
