Welcome Dashboards

UX UI B2B SAAS E-MOBILITY ANGULAR

We improved the efficiency of the eRoaming portal welcome pages by transforming them into actionable dashboards, expanding their functionalities from 5 to over 20. This redesign significantly improvs clarity and enables both primary user types to access critical information more quickly, leading to better and faster problem-solving.

Hubject's product offers the largest EV-charging network, helping B2B players: CPOs (charging point operators) and EMPs (electric mobility providers) build lasting connections for a smooth charging experience for EV drivers.

Product background

The Hubject eRoaming product offers a platform with tools to enable users manage both business and technical aspects of their EV charging networks.
Key functionalities of the portal include:
  •  Form business connections:
      -  create charging contracts and update tariffs,
      -  nagotiate and subscribe to avilable contracts,
      -  discover new partners.
  •  Establish technical connections:
      -  transfer charging data,
      -  monitor sucessful or failed charging sessions.

The welcome page

The original portal welcome page (see image below) offers each user type—CPOs and EMPs—access to relevant applications and several common widgets, including an onboarding card, feedback option, and a link to the previous portal version.

The original Hubject eRoaming welcome page - EMP user

The problem

The welcome page was failing to deliver value to users. It featured only 4-5 static widgets that provided minimal new information, leaving users frustrated when attempting to navigate the portal effectively. Additionally, the design lacked alignment with the product's design system.

CPO and EMP eRoaming managers struggle to efficiently address urgent matters (like expiring contracts, charging issues or reaching out to partners), due to unclear navigation and a low-value welcome page.

Our hypothesis was that replacing the welcome page with a dashboard could provide users with more actionable value and improve their overall experience.

CPO and EMP eRoaming managers quotes from past research

The main challenge

The core challenge lay in determining which insights and information users needed most to take action and address their urgent tasks effectively, while ensuring clarity and orientation in the dashboard's layout.

Identifying the right valuable data for users was a challenge due to the platform's data-heavy nature. While it holds a broad range of data points serving various purposes, not all of this information can or should be shared with users. Moreover, individual data points can be visualized in multiple ways, each offering different insights to users.

Project kick-off

We kicked off the project with a collaborative design sprint, focusing on defining clear objectives that would serve as a solid starting point for future iterations and user validation.
In the end of the workshop, we aligned on targeting both CPO and EMP eRoaming managers and designing a hybrid dashboard that combines analytical and operational elements.

Kick-off design sprint objectives summary

The process and approach

To address the valuable insight identification challenge, we adopted an exploration and refinement approach to eventually uncover the right solutions.
After defining the objectives, the PO and I conducted further research to expand our concept options using the following methods: data inventory, dashboard benchmarking and product best practices research. Later we regrouped to share insights and summarize a dashboard concept.

I then began exploring widget concepts for user testing, focusing on low-fi variations in Miro. Considering the limited user testing opportunities and tight timelines, I explored multiple widget versions per topic to quickly generate ideas and align with the PO on the most promising concepts.

B2B Dashboard design benchmarking: Best practices and insights

User testing

I collaborated with my team to refine the testing approach, agreeing to test two dashboard versions per user type.
Key concept test goals:
  •  Evaluating the hypothesis for dashboard type: present two dashboard versions per user type, and asked users to rate their preferred dashboard on a scale of 1-5..
  •  Identify valuable data and widget preferences: exposing users to a variety of widgets perdashboard version, and gathering detailed feedback, comments and responses on which widgets provided the most value.

We performed concept tests with 3 CPO partners, and 2 EMP partners.
The tests generated valuable insights: while users rated KPI-focused dashboards higher, they indicated that operational widgets would provide more value in their daily tasks. Based on the feedback, we concluded that by combining both analytical and operational approaches we could deliver the most impactful solution.

Cluster finding and reiterate

We clustered user feedback from the tests, highlighting key comments, pain points, and wishes. These insights were used to refine widget concepts and reassess their importance, identifying must-haves, nice-to-haves, and elements to remove.

Based on user needs, widgets were improved, and I researched dashboard layouts best practices to refine the user story and priorities. This helped ensure critical widgets were positioned prominently to meet users' highest priorities.

Dashboard storytelling

The next step was to define the layout and widget order within the dashboard.
I conducted additional research on dashboard layout best practices, focusing on data visualization and technical structure.

My final challenge was to establish the hierarchy of information—deciding which widgets should appear at the top and which could be secondary. To address this, I revisited the personas' tasks, pain points, and core motivations, using dashboard storytelling to clarify the importance and order of each widget.

Next steps

The dashboard designs for each user type are now complete as high-fidelity wireframes in Figma, incorporating interactions aligned with user flows and addressing UI edge cases such as narrow or wide screens and widget empty states.

The next steps involve conducting another round of usability testing on the refined solution, focusing on user behavior during urgent task completion tests. Based on the feedback, we will further refine the designs before preparing the screens for a development handover.

The outcome

The solution we implemented had a significant positive impact on both the business and user experience. We successfully achieved our business goals by increasong the charging transactions rate by 65%, which directly contributed to our users' success.

Users feedback to the prototype testing was positive, appreciating the impact on their daily work, while raising valuable feedback and suggestions for improvement we are considering for future iterations.
