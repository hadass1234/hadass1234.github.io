I designed the end-to-end invoice management workflow for a new B2B financial services product.
The system solution automated invoices creation, ensured data accuracy, and supported easy payments and payouts, while meeting regulatory compliance requirements.

🚙 Financial challenges in the EV Industry

B2B sellers in the EV charging space face frequent conflicts with their B2B clients due to a lack of invoice data transparency and slow dispute resolution, resulting in delayed payments and revenue loss.


🎯 Business goal

The business requested a new product offering a complete financial solution tailored for the EV charging industry.

The product goal is to streamline the entire invoicing and payment lifecycle for B2B sellers, from charging data to final payout, while ensuring partners remain compliant with payment regulatory standard.

🚩 The Problem

B2B Seller companies faced challenges when managing efficient invoices management:
📂   Error-prone data transfer from the charging department to finance is manual and creates delays.
📩   Low transperancy when sharing invoices with clients causing slow and manual verification and payment processing.
🕵   Manual dispute resolution current process is time-consuming and inefficient.

These inefficiencies slowed down monthly operations and created friction between finance and energy managers.

🚀 Project kick-off

I began tackling the invoicing project in more depth together with the dedicated Product Owner, after we, as a team (3 Product Owners and 2 UX/UI designers), had refined the overall product concept, defined the initial scope and user flow, and clarified the data structure in collaboration with the development team.


⛰️ My Challenge

The biggest challenge was designing a solution that addressed the needs of two distinct roles: financial managers and charging managers. Both roles had their own workflows, and I needed to create a product that streamlined and simplified both processes into a single, cohesive solution.

With limited access to the legal team due to business constraints, I had to find ways to meet legal and regulatory requirements using alternative research methods while ensuring the system aligned with the industry’s standards.
