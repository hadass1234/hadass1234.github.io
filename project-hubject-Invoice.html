I designed the end-to-end invoice management workflow for a new B2B financial services product.

💵 Financial challenges in the EV Industry

B2B sellers in the EV charging space face frequent conflicts with their B2B clients due to a lack of invoice data transparency and slow dispute resolution, resulting in delayed payments and revenue loss.


🎯 Business goal

The business requested a new product offering a complete financial solution tailored for the EV charging industry.

The product goal is to streamline the entire invoicing and payment lifecycle for B2B sellers, from charging data to final payout, while ensuring partners remain compliant with payment regulatory standard.

caption herer

🚩 The Problem

B2B Seller companies faced challenges when managing efficient invoices management:
1. Error-prone data transfer from the charging department to finance is manual and creates delays.
2. Low transperancy when sharing invoices with clients causing slow and manual verification and payment processing.
3. Inefficient dispute resolution current process is time-consuming and very manual.

These inefficiencies slowed down monthly operations and created friction between finance and energy managers.

🏃‍♀️‍➡️ Project kick-off

I began tackling the invoicing project in depth together with the dedicated Product Owner, after we as a product team (3 Product Owners and 2 UX/UI designers), had refined the overall product concept, defined the initial scope and user flow, and clarified the data structure in collaboration with the development team.

HMW design an end-to-end invoicing solutionthat meets legal standards while ensuring data accuracy in the EV charging industry?


⛰️ My Challenge

The biggest challenge was designing a unified solution that supported the distinct tasks of two different roles in the current invoicing workflow: financial managers and charging managers.
Another constrain was limited access to the legal team, so we had to find alternative ways to meet legal and regulatory requirements.

caption herer

🔎 Discovery and research

I used UX research methods to better understand financial flows and invoice structures.
While conducting SME interview with a financial employee I observed their workflows to learn the common tasks and behaviour. In addition, I conducted an extensive competitor analysis of B2B billing, invoicing, and payment systems, to study best practices of financial teams.

caption herer

💡 Key insight

I discovered users place a strong emphasis on data verification, given the legal nature of invoices. After uncovering this core users need, I began drafting the information structure for the invoice view, focusing on clarity, accuracy, and traceability to support the verification process.


📝 Validate flows with users

As a team, we conducted concept testing to validate key flows, such as invoice approval, revocation, and credit note creation. I prepared and facilitated some of the tests, participated in the sessions, and took detailed notes. User feedback provided valuable insights, which we used to refine interactions and improve clarity for the final MVP design.

caption herer

🚀 The solution design

The final result is a centralized invoice view that prioritizes data clarity and simplifies verification tasks.

The design solution brings together all key information—line items, recipient details, revenue breakdown, and linked charging sessions—within a clear and organized layout.

The system also enables users to track invoice status, export data in various formats, and resolve disputes faster through a credit note process based on individual charging sessions.

caption herer

📈 The outcome

The solution was successfully released at the end of 2024, with 4 partners onboarded for the pilot phase.

A partner who had been using the product for 2 months emailed us with positive feedback, praising the simple and user-friendly design (see quote below)
This feedback, among other feedbacks we are collectiong through follow-up interviews, shows how the solution meets users' need for clear and easy access to all the data they need. It also improves the accuracy of data calculations and transfers, solving key issues with reliable and transparent information.
