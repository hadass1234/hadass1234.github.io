Hubject — 2023
B2B Partner finder and partner business profile

UX UI B2B E-MOBILITY MARKETPLACE ANGULAR

Hubject provides the largest EV-charging marketplace platform, enabling CPOs (Charging points operators) and EMPs (B2C E-Mobility providers) to create long-lasting business and technical connections for seamless electric charging experience for end EV drivers customers.

Our team increased charging session rates and significantly helped EMP partners to easily expand their charging stations network by creating a CPO-partner search and a detailed partner profile view.

My role: Product designer intern (end-to-end UX and UI design)
Duration: 3 Months
Team: 2 Product designers, 1 Product owner, 2 FE developers, 2 BE developers
Credits: Hubject GmbH

New CPO finder

The product before

The growing digital product - a B2B eRoaming platform, enables EMP partners to technically connect to CPO partners' charging station networks by subscribing to their avilable contract, so EV drivers could access and charge their electric cars.

Business goal and assumptions

Business requierment: increase charging sessions made via the platform.

We assumed that by improving the number of contracts partners make in order to expand their charging network, will eventually increase number of charging transactions.

We interviewd EMP partners to understand what are they looking for when expanding their network, in order to find out how to improve the product.

Persona quotes based on user interviews

Users need more in-platform information

Discovery phase taught us that each EMP has their own busines strategy that aligns with their target EV drivers.

Since expanding charging network is a major task, we concluded that we should enable EMP partners a suitable search engine and more detailed information on the CPO partners they'd like to connect with.


Design sprint to solution

Next step was to explore the best search flow for B2B Marketplaces.
During a 3-days design sprint, we have iterated on search practices that fits out product constrains, and generated a flow prototype we have tested with EMP partners.

The Users


According to the initial briefing interview with toshi, it is established the app targets users with the following characteristics:

Demographics: age 18-42 years old; live in Germany; all genders (but prefer focus on women).
Financial characteristics: Prefer cashless payment; have no prior investment experience or knowledge.

The Research


Research methods applied:
  •  Secondary research
  •  Competitors analysis
  •  8 x Interviews (2 SME, 6 Users)
  •  2 x Usability testing (on existing toshi's app)

The user interviews exposed their pain points around finances, savings, investments and retirement.
We discovered users are scared of losing their money, do not trust traditional financial players, yet are aware of the importance of taking care of their retirement. Also, we discovered they would like to educate themselves about investment, but are overwhelmed by the financial terms and stock market unstable behaviour.

Through the SME interviews, we clarified the importance of having a clear financial goal, and keeping a calm, long-term strategy mindset.

Ideating solutions


Considering the users' needs and pain points, we practiced brainstorming and crazy 8 methods and explored new ways to display the traditional investment portfolio

Design solutions we explored:
  •  Rethink what the users actually need to know in order to make an informed decision
  •  Bridge financial terms
  •  Model retirement as a tangible goal
  •  Separate investment strategy from happenings report.
  •  Add encouraging steps to engage the users

Following multiple prototyping and testing rounds, we created a new holistic design flow of the portfolio overview.

The Outcome


During the High-fi testing, users expressed more trust in the app as a financial tool, more understanding of their portfolio progress, and general calmness during the interaction.

toshi’s team integrated core insights from our research and new experience design features in their MVP launch:
embedded the tangible goal visualizations, the objective info and expert advice tabs, and more transparency about the portfolio breakdown.

The Design


toshi's team was open to a redesign of the app. Therefore, in order to better enhance the brand values and sense of reliability and security for the users, I've rebranded toshi's app, inspired by contemporary app design and using material design components.
