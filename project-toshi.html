Invest with every shopping

UX/UI DESIGN THINKING ANDROID ART DIRECTION GRAPHIC DESIGN

toshi is a German based startup that helps users invest money for their retirement. With toshi's automated app, for every cashless purchase the user makes, a certain precentage will be invested in a long-term portfolio.

The challenge: redesign the investment portfolio dashboard, include all necessary information and make is easy to understand for people who did not invest before.

First place winner project in Ironhack Germany hackshow DEC 2022 (UX/UI category)

Responsibilities: Research, UX Design, Strategy, Prototyping, Art Direction, Graphic Design.
Credits: Ironhack x toshi. Team-partner: Amanda Liedberg

The Strategy
In order to redesign the portfolio in a way that will be understandable and for the users, we decided use design thinking research, and focus methods that will generate qualitative findings. Our plan was to seek pain points and reveal the real fears of the users. Later, we will define 1-2 personas, and ideate on new solutions that will address their needs.

The Results
We found the design opportunities with the highest potential of addressing user's pain points are in bridging the information and managing potentially stressful situations.

Our solution:
• Visually redesign the app, in a way that will communicate more stability and warmth
• Rearrange the structure of a typical portfolio overview
• Use an objective tone of language, as well as translate all terms to the user's financial level
• Add tips, clarification, and guidance
• Make the long-term goal more tangible

toshi's motivation
In Germany, 1 of every 5 people over 65 years old are at risk to live in poverty.
toshi’s goal is to help users with no prior investment experience, to invest their money in a long-term investment portfolio by using an automation process, and save money for their retirement.

How it works?
For every cashless shopping the user makes, the app will automatically invest a certain percentage of the amount into a long-term investment portfolio.

The Porblem
toshi’s team raised their main issue:
"How can we design a portfolio overview which has all the necessary information and is easy to understand for people who did not invest before."

They described the forms of research they performed, and mentioned the areas that require improvements:
1. Trust & Security
The users’ main issue was mistrust in the app, as well as expressing confusion and frustration while reviewing the portfolio, raising questions such as “where is my money?”.
2. Address the target group
While a main challenge is that toshi needs to display investment information to users with no prior investment knowledge, they do not wish to become an educational platform, nor do they believe their target users wish to educate themselves. They wish to remain a tool.
3. Look & Visuals
toshi were open for a new visual make-over, that will communicate their current brand attributes: Flexibility, Transparency & Simplicity.

Empatize: The Research
In order to understand the topic and the users better, we conducted different research methods:
• Secondary research & Market research
• 2 x SME interview with a financial advisor and financial expert
• 6 x Interviews with potential users
• 2 x Usability tests on the entire app flow (On-boarding + Portfolio overview) with potential users

Why?
From our deep interview with toshi, it became clear that the main issues are placed in an abstract problem - trust.
Therefore, our approach was to use qualitative methods, “dig deeper” and get a better understanding of the user’s point of view and actual fears regarding investments and finances. Once we empathize better with the user, we will be able to find a solution that will address their needs.

In addition, we conducted SME interviews, secondary research, and market research so we could learn which information is necessary to display and figure out how we can better position toshi compared to their competitors.

Define & Focus
We gathered all the information and insights into an affinity diagram. We discovered different design opportunities, and carefully evaluated and decided to focus on 3-4 topics we knew we could solve within the scope of the project, and will be manageable in the short time frame we had.

The topics we focused on:
💸 - Finance scares me
😨 - Stress related behavior
👀 - Create trust
💎 - Features (nice to have)

Personas
Based on the patterns and pain points we recognized in the research phase, we created 2 personas to focus on. Even though they have a lot in common, each has different motivations and might respond differently regarding investment and risks.

Testing
After establishing the new layout and design, we tested the prototypes on 5 different potential users.
Through testing we discovered several misunderstandings, and it gave us the opportunity to fix and improve.

Yet, we also noticed the users were engaging more naturally compared to when using the original design, and they were satisfied with reviewing the portfolio on its first screens only. The testing revealed the testes didn’t search or tried to explore the technical stock-market charts, which confirmed our assumption that the information should indeed be bridged to the users, in their own language.
