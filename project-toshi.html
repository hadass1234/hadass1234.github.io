toshi — 2022
Invest with every shopping

UX UI ANDROID APP ART DIRECTION

toshi is a German based startup that helps users invest money for their retirement. With toshi's automated app, for every cashless purchase the user makes, a certain precentage is invested in a long-term portfolio. 🏆 First place winner project in Ironhack Germany hackshow Dec 2022 (UX/UI category)

Our team helped toshi improve their current app, by uncovering the users' financial-related pain points and redesigning the traditional investment portfolio for users who did not invest before.

My responsibilities: UX Research, UX Design, Prototyping, Art Direction, UI Design
Duration: 2 weeks
Team: 2 product designers (Amanda Liedberg & Hadass Sternberg)
Credits: Ironhack Berlin, toshi

The Problem


toshi’s team reported they struggle to engage the users with the app due to their lack of trust in the product, and lack of basic investments understanding.

The raised question: How can we redesign a portfolio overview which has all necessary information and is easy to understand for people who did not invest before?

The Users


According to the initial briefing interview with toshi, it is established the app targets users with the following characteristics:

Demographics: age 18-42 years old; live in Germany; all genders (but prefer focus on women).
Financial characteristics: Prefer cashless payment; have no prior investment experience or knowledge.

The Research


Research methods applied:
  •  Secondary research
  •  Competitors analysis
  •  8 x Interviews (2 SME, 6 Users)
  •  2 x Usability testing (on existing toshi's app)

The User interviews exposed their pain points around finances, savings, investments and retirement. We discovered they are scared of losing their money, do not trust traditional financial players, yet are aware of the importance of taking care of their retirement. Also, we discovered they would like to educate themselves about investment, but are overwhelmed by the financial terms and stock market unstable behaviour.

Through the SME interviews, we clarified the importance of having a clear financial goal, and keeping a calm, long-term strategy mindset.

The Design oportunities


Our main pain-points, insights and design opportunities that guided our solutions were:
  •  Create trust - which we solved through high-level of transparency and objective tone of voice
  •  Stressful situations - we address by guiding and reminding the users of their long-term goal
  •  Bridge the knowledge - which we solved by adding an educational section and translation of financial terms to user’s financial level, throughout the entire app.

Following multiple prototyping and testing rounds, we created a new holistic design flow of the portfolio overview.
In addition, we visually rebranded toshi's app in order to create a stronger sense of reliability and warmth.
