Toshi — 2022
Invest with every shopping

UX UI B2C FINTECH ANDROID APP

Toshi is a German based startup that helps users invest money for their retirement. With Toshi's automated app, for every cashless purchase the user makes, a certain precentage is invested in a long-term portfolio. 🏆 First place winner project in Ironhack Germany hackshow Dec 2022 (UX/UI category)

Our team helped Toshi improve their current app by uncovering the users' financial-related pain points, and redesigning the traditional investment portfolio for users who did not invest before.

My responsibilities: UX Research, UX Design, Prototyping, Art Direction, UI Design
Duration: 2 weeks
Team: 2 product designers
Credits: Ironhack Berlin, Toshi

The problem and target users

Toshi's founders shared that they are having trouble engaging users with the app. They reported users expressed a lack of trust in the app, but couldn't track back to the exact reason.

The Toshi team seeked a user experience improvment, and was open for a branding/interface redesign.

The product's target group is users aged 18-42 years old; who live in Germany; all genders. They prefer cashless payment and have no prior investment experience or knowledge.

Original app usability test findings

How might we engage users?

Conducting multiple usability tests on the original Toshi app helped us better understand the fraction points that caused users to not engage with the app.
To docover users' needs and pain points related to finances and investing, we conducted qualitative research in the form of interviews with both potential users and SME in the financial field.

To focus our efforts, we defined our goal:
How can we redesign a portfolio overview which has all necessary information and is easy to understand for people who did not invest before?

User interviews KPIs

Financial pain points of non-investors

Research taught us that users who have no stock-market investment experience are very curious on investing money and understand it's value. Yet, they are driven by fears of market instability, not understnding financial terms, or being cheated.

Simultaneously, the SME interview taught us the foundations for good investment behavior - creating a long-term investment strategy, staying calm and focusing on the end goal, and investing steadily but consistently. All points can be defined by knowing one's risk tolerance and financial knowledge.

Users' pain points and design oportunity

The Solution: Simplifying investment for beginners

According to our findings and insights, we approached the task of designing an investment portfolio overview in a more open-minded approach that doesn't necessarly focuses on delivering financial facts - but bridging the information in a tangiable way for users.

Among our top priority features were: Communicating long-term retirment plan in a tangiable way, encouraging users to invest with setting investment goals, adding guiding tips in moments of market drop, adding a basics educational section, and more.

toshi app redesign

Action driven home screen

Toshi's original home screen displayed the investment portfolio value and trend, as well as upcoming investment amounts, investment settings, and a large invest CTA button.

Our new design focused on encouraging users to engage with their long-term goal - saving for retirement. We added an house illustration to the portfolio main card, to make the goal more tangible. In addition, we added an investment progress bar with their ideal investment monthly goal. A new article features linking to a financial educational section in the app, encouraging the users to learn more about finances in smaller knowledge doses, and kept the large CTA button.

Primary portfolio card

New home screen

Clear transaction overview

In the original transactions screen, users could see the different deposit amounts and terms they didn't understand the logic behind. Also, the transfer history was grouped in a a way that confused the users and got them frustrated.

Our new design for the screen focused on transperancy and clarity. We rephrased some terms, and added explanatory info bubbles. Users could now easily see the amount they have invested, and history of investments grouped by months, and chronological order.

New transactions screen

Transactions history section

Portfolio insights over data

Instead of displaying stock-market graphs and charts that can be consumed with the right financial knowledge, we reconsidered the way we show the investment state to users with no prior investment experience.

Users wishing to look into their portfolio will see the total amounts viewed with gain or loss, then their investment settings, long-term goal progress, and breakdown of sectors they invest in. If users would like to get into more details, they could enter a "live portfolio" and see the actual movement of their portfolio in the stock market.

Ideating solutions


Considering the users' needs and pain points, we practiced brainstorming and crazy 8 methods and explored new ways to display the traditional investment portfolio

Design solutions we explored:
  •  Rethink what the users actually need to know in order to make an informed decision
  •  Bridge financial terms
  •  Model retirement as a tangible goal
  •  Separate investment strategy from happenings report.
  •  Add encouraging steps to engage the users

Following multiple prototyping and testing rounds, we created a new holistic design flow of the portfolio overview.

The Outcome


During the High-fi testing, users expressed more trust in the app as a financial tool, more understanding of their portfolio progress, and general calmness during the interaction.

Toshi’s team integrated core insights from our research and new experience design features in their MVP launch:
embedded the tangible goal visualizations, the objective info and expert advice tabs, and more transparency about the portfolio breakdown.

The Design


Toshi's team was open to a redesign of the app. Therefore, in order to better enhance the brand values and sense of reliability and security for the users, I've rebranded Toshi's app, inspired by contemporary app design and using material design components.
